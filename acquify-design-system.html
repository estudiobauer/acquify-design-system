08 / Sections
01 / Principles

Five rules that govern everything.

The brand is predominantly dark. Light emerges from within, never imposed from outside. These five principles precede every design decision — from a single ad creative to a full product UI.

01
Dark base
Light emerges from within the composition, not laid on top of it.
02
Organic structure
No mechanical grids. Form follows breath, not measurement.
03
Selective luminosity
Brightness is earned. Reserve light for what matters most.
04
Volume and depth
Flat is not Acquify. Every surface has weight, atmosphere, and material.
05
Monochromatic range
Black to off-white. No chromatic accents. Ever.

04 / Typography

Two voices. Never inverted.

06 / 3D Elements

Volumetric objects as focal anchors.

3D elements give compositions weight, gravity, and depth. Always rendered in monochrome — chrome, matte black, or off-white materials only. Used sparingly as the singular focal point of a layout.

01
Chrome Orb · Hero
02
Hex Module · Product
03
Apex · Brand Mark 3D
Material rules. 3D elements are rendered in three approved materials: chrome/liquid metal (highly reflective, polished — for hero compositions), matte black with sharp edge-light (industrial, brand-mark module — for product/feature visuals), and off-white satin (rare, ceremonial — for premium announcements). Always cast soft drop shadows. Never use colored lighting, neon rims, or decorative environments. One 3D object per layout — they are focal points, not decoration.
